# Banking-Operations-Dashboard-Power-BI
📌 Project Overview
This Power BI dashboard analyzes banking operations data across 729 customers and 4 banking relationship types — Commercial, Institutional, Private Bank, and Retail. The goal was to understand deposit vs loan distribution, customer segmentation, risk exposure, and account balance trends over 3 decades (1990–2020).

📊 Key Metrics Tracked

Total Customers      729

🔍 Key Insights from the Dashboard

Loan Portfolio Risk: Business loans dominate at higher risk weightings — risk score peaks around the $2M–$4M loan range, suggesting mid-size business loans carry the most exposure
Account Balance Distribution: Majority of account balance (52.9% = $231.83M) falls in the largest segment, while only 5.05% ($22.12M) is in the smallest — showing high concentration risk
Customer Segmentation: Gold loyalty customers hold the highest share of total value at 41.82% ($52.09M), while Platinum customers remain underrepresented — opportunity for upselling
Gender Split: Nearly equal distribution — 51.99% male (379), 48.01% female (350) — useful for targeted product design
Customer Growth Trend: Customer acquisition peaked around 2005–2015, with visible decline post-2015 — signals need for retention strategy
Risk Weighting: Average risk score of 1654 (out of 3308 max) — portfolio sits at moderate risk level overall


🛠️ Tools & Techniques Used

Tool: Microsoft Power BI Desktop
Data Modeling: Star schema with customer, account, and loan tables
DAX Measures: SUM, CALCULATE, DIVIDE for KPI cards
Visuals Used: Donut chart, gauge chart, scatter plot, bar chart, pie chart, slicers
Filters/Slicers: Banking relationship type (Commercial / Institutional / Private Bank / Retail)


💼 Business Use Case
This dashboard is designed for bank executives and risk analysts to:

Monitor overall financial health of the portfolio
Identify high-risk loan segments before they default
Track customer loyalty distribution for retention campaigns
Analyze 30-year customer growth trends for strategic planning
